I'm John Dornstadter — a senior at Texas A&M's Mays Business School studying Business Marketing with a focus on Advertising Strategy. But if I'm honest, most of what I know didn't come from a classroom.

I started my real estate career at 19 while still in college. No referrals, no network, no head start. I built my pipeline from scratch using TikTok, Facebook, and relentless follow-up — and turned that into nearly $9 million in closed volume across 40 transaction sides. That experience gave me something most marketing students don't have: real stakes. Every ad dollar I spent was my own money. Every lead that didn't convert was a commission lost.

That mindset carried into everything else I've taken on. At FYZICAL Physical Therapy, I managed a full-stack marketing operation — running Google and Facebook ads, personally building physician referral relationships, and orchestrating a community event that drew 50+ cold attendees. In eight months, I drove 392 patient referrals and grew monthly volume by 300%. At Transmission Lost, I managed social media and paid campaigns that grew Spotify monthly listeners from 5,200 to 8,500+.

What drives me is the problem of conversion — how do you take someone who doesn't know you exist and turn them into a client, a patient, a fan? That question sits at the intersection of psychology, data, and creative execution. I'm drawn to it every time.

Most recently, I built CloseCast — a fully published iOS mobile app available on the Apple App Store, built with React, TypeScript, Firebase, and Gemini AI. I built it because the tools I needed didn't exist. Using Xcode to ship it through Apple's review process end-to-end, with real offline sync, intelligent CSV importing, and two-way Todoist integration. I didn't have a background in software engineering. I just had a problem to solve and enough stubbornness to solve it.

I'm looking for opportunities in marketing strategy, growth, or brand management — ideally in a role where data informs creative, and creative drives real outcomes. If you're building something that needs a marketer who has actually run the plays, I'd love to talk.
